Engagement Models

We offer flexible engagement structures to fit different project needs.

Fixed-Scope Projects

Clearly defined deliverables with agreed-upon scope and pricing. Suitable for projects with well-understood requirements.

Time & Materials

Flexible engagement for evolving requirements. You pay for actual work performed, with regular reporting and transparency.

Dedicated Team

A team allocated to your project for extended periods. Suitable for ongoing product development or long-term initiatives.

Retainer / Support

Ongoing support agreement for maintenance, bug fixes, and small enhancements. Predictable monthly commitment.

What to Expect

Here's how initial conversations typically go.

1

Initial Contact

You reach out via email or the contact form. Tell us about your project, timeline, and any specific requirements.

2

Discovery Call

We schedule a call to understand your project in more depth. We'll ask questions about the business problem, users, technical constraints, and success criteria.

3

Assessment

We evaluate the project scope, complexity, and fit with our capabilities. For some projects, we may request additional documentation or a follow-up session.

4

Proposal

If there's a good fit, we provide a proposal outlining scope, approach, timeline, and pricing. No obligation—you decide if you want to proceed.

Common Questions

What size projects do you take on?

We work on projects ranging from MVPs to large enterprise systems. The key factor is whether the project is a good fit for our team's capabilities and approach.

Do you work with clients remotely?

Yes. Most of our client relationships are remote. We use video calls, shared documentation, and collaboration tools to stay connected.

Can you work with our existing team?

Yes. We can integrate with your internal team, work alongside other vendors, or take full ownership depending on what makes sense for your situation.

Do you sign NDAs?

Yes. We understand that some project details are confidential. We're happy to sign an NDA before detailed discussions.

Who owns the code?

You do. All code we write for your project is your intellectual property. We use standard work-for-hire agreements.

What about ongoing support?

We offer ongoing support agreements for projects we've built. We can also provide documentation and knowledge transfer if you prefer to bring support in-house.
